They come rushing in. Sometimes too fast. Sometimes very slow.

I have always loved trains and railway stations. There is something about them that just draws me in.

When it comes to booking train tickets, travellers fall into three types: those who plan ahead and book well in advance, those who prefer the flexibility of a general ticket, and then there are procrastinators like me who end up on Tatkal. Booking a Tatkal ticket feels like qualifying JEE and getting into IIT Bombay — stressful, competitive, and somehow satisfying when it works.

India runs approximately 12,617 trains carrying over 23 million passengers daily. That is roughly the entire population of Australia, moving across the country every single day.

I usually travel in Sleeper Class for longer journeys, and in AC coaches when visiting my in-laws in Mysore. But Sleeper Class is where it gets interesting. It is a true reflection of India — people from every religion, culture, language, and background sharing the same berths, the same food smells, the same delays. A word of advice though: avoid seats 1–8 or 60–72 if you are particular about aromas.

The more I travel by train, the more I see it as a metaphor for life. Different classes offer different comfort levels, but everyone is heading somewhere. Stations are like milestones — some planned, some unexpected. Unscheduled stops happen in life too, and you just have to wait them out.

As James Thomson wrote — the stars keep shining overhead, regardless of which class you are travelling in.

Next time you are on a sleeper berth, stuck between a snoring stranger and a family sharing a tiffin box, look out the window. There is something beautiful about it.
